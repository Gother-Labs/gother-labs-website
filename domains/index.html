Optimization domains where better decisions must be governed.

We work where measurable improvement, operating constraints, and technical trust have to move together.

01 Algorithmic Performance

Improve the code paths that decide the system's ceiling.

Some systems are limited by a small number of kernels, heuristics, numerical routines, or evaluation harnesses. We target the code paths where performance, reliability, cost, and correctness are tightly coupled.

Progress here has to be benchmarkable. A change should survive regression checks, explain its tradeoffs, and make the implementation easier to trust rather than merely faster in isolation.

02 Scientific Computing

Make computational experiments faster, clearer, and reproducible.

Scientific computing often depends on simulation, calibration, uncertainty, and chaotic dynamics where intuition is not enough. We focus on workflows where experimental speed and methodological control have to improve together.

The aim is a research loop that is easier to reproduce, easier to compare, and more precise about why a model, method, or parameter choice deserves confidence.

03 Energy & Renewables

Dispatch, storage, and grid-aware decision loops.

Energy systems are shaped by forecasts, market signals, physical constraints, and asset state that change faster than static playbooks. We focus on decision loops where dispatch, storage, planning, and scenario evaluation need to be improved without losing auditability.

The goal is not a black-box recommendation. It is a governed operating policy that can show why it improves outcomes, where it holds, and how it behaves when conditions move.

04 Operational Planning

Scheduling, routing, allocation, and throughput under real constraints.

Operational planning is where many small decisions compound across assets, teams, queues, and service commitments. We work on the planning surfaces where routing, allocation, capacity, and utilization need to be optimized against constraints that are not optional.

Better planning should make tradeoffs visible, reduce avoidable friction, and remain resilient when demand, resources, or priorities change.
