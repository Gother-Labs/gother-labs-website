What to send

A short note with relevant context is enough. The most useful messages usually include a concrete problem surface, a link, artifact, or a concise explanation of why the kernel matters and where the current behavior breaks down.

Collaborations and research

If you want to discuss a collaboration, research partnership, or a kernel that deserves serious evaluation, use the same channel. We would rather start from the technical or operational reality than from a polished pitch.

Careers

If you think you are unusually aligned with how we work, write to the same inbox. Relevant context, work samples, or a concise case for fit is more useful than a formal application. We care more about judgment, rigor, and operating taste than about volume.
