Göther makes important kernels governable.

We work in the gap between tolerated legacy behavior and what becomes achievable when a kernel is evaluated under explicit constraints and taken seriously.

What We Are Building

Evölther is the internal system behind this work.

We use the name Evölther for the governed improvement loop that sits behind our work on important kernels: constraints stay explicit, evidence remains inspectable, and deployment is a decision rather than a vibe.

It matters to what Göther is building, but it is not yet a public product surface. For now, the right level of visibility is inside the company story, not as a standalone public page.

What Göther is

Göther Labs is a research-driven team building systems that search for better policies, heuristics, and code under an executable definition of success.

What we optimize for

We prefer bounded kernels, frozen baselines, explicit constraints, and runs that leave behind artifacts. Better should be inspectable, not rhetorical.

Why this matters

Many organizations carry optimization debt: important algorithms drift, get patched locally, and stop being seriously re-evaluated. A governed loop turns that drift into a disciplined search problem.
